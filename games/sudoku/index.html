How to Play Sudoku

The goal is simple: fill every cell in the 9x9 grid so that each row, each column, and each 3x3 box contains the digits 1 through 9 exactly once. No repeats allowed. Start with the given numbers and use logic to deduce the rest.

Tap or click a cell to select it, then choose a number. Use pencil mode to jot down candidates when you're not sure. Undo any mistakes with a single tap, and let the auto-save handle the rest so you can pick up right where you left off.

Difficulty Levels

  • 🌿
    Easy
    38+ given digits. Perfect for beginners or a relaxing session. Most cells can be solved with basic elimination.
  • 🌞
    Medium
    30-37 given digits. Requires more scanning and occasional pencil marks. A satisfying challenge for regular players.
  • 🔥
    Hard
    25-29 given digits. You'll need advanced techniques like naked pairs, hidden singles, and pointing pairs.
  • 💀
    Expert
    22-24 given digits. For serious solvers. X-Wing, Swordfish, and chain logic may be required. Good luck.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Sudoku free to play?
Yes, completely free. No ads, no in-app purchases, no downloads. Just open the page and start solving.
Can I play on my phone or tablet?
Absolutely. The Sudoku grid is designed for touch input on any screen size. It works beautifully on phones, tablets, and desktops alike.
What are the difficulty levels?
There are four: Easy (38+ givens), Medium (30-37 givens), Hard (25-29 givens), and Expert (22-24 givens). Each level requires progressively more advanced solving techniques.
What are pencil marks and how do I use them?
Pencil marks let you note possible candidate numbers in a cell before committing. Toggle pencil mode, then tap numbers to add or remove them. It's the classic technique every serious Sudoku player uses.
Is there a timer?
Yes, every puzzle is timed. Your fastest solve times are saved locally and submitted to the global leaderboard when you're signed in. Compete for the fastest time on each difficulty level.
