One Stroke — One-Line Drawing Puzzle Game

Play One Stroke free in your browser — a clean, modern take on the classic one-line drawing puzzle. Each board is a Hamiltonian path challenge: visit every playable node exactly once in a single connected stroke. With 200 campaign levels, Daily and Weekly leaderboards, Bonus Paths, friend challenges, mastery medals and 31 trophies, it's easy to pick up and genuinely hard to put down. No download, no signup — just draw.

How to Play One Stroke

Drag from the highlighted start node to any neighbouring node to begin your path. Keep extending the line one step at a time — the level is solved the moment every node has been visited exactly once. Campaign levels normally have a free end; the optional Bonus Paths event adds a marked goal and a limited undo budget.

  • Drag (mouse or finger) from node to node — orthogonal steps only.
  • Drag back along your path, or press Z, to undo one step.
  • R resets the level, H shows a hint for the next recommended node, N jumps to the next level.
  • Hints are free in campaign mode but cost score in the daily challenge.

200 Campaign Levels, Four Difficulties

The campaign takes you from gentle warm-up grids to genuinely brain-bending boards across four difficulty tiers: Easy, Medium, Hard and Very Hard. A full level-select screen lets you search and filter every level by difficulty and status. Bronze, silver and gold mastery medals reward cleaner clears, while best time and performance stats are tracked as you go.

Daily Challenge & Streaks

Every day brings a fresh set of five levels that are the same for every player in the world. Your first complete run is the ranked attempt; later replays are clearly marked practice. Compare score and time, build a streak and review the last seven UTC days. Weekly Challenge adds a longer ten-level run, while friend codes enable split-by-split head-to-head play.

Trophies & Leaderboards

One Stroke includes 31 trophies across four tiers — 15 bronze, 10 silver, 5 gold and 1 platinum — covering campaign milestones, challenge feats and everything in between. Your challenge runs are saved to a local high-score history with detailed per-run stats, and signing in to GameVolt syncs your achievements across devices.

Plays Great on Mobile

One Stroke is built mobile-first. Draw your path with a fingertip, use the bottom tab bar to flip between the board, menu and stats, and reach Undo, Reset, Hint and Next from dedicated touch controls right below the board. The grid scales to any screen size, from phone to desktop.

One Stroke FAQ

Is One Stroke free to play?

Yes. One Stroke is 100% free in your browser with no download and no signup. Sign in to GameVolt only if you want your trophies and progress synced across devices.

What kind of puzzle is One Stroke?

It's a Hamiltonian path puzzle, often called a one-line or one-stroke drawing puzzle. You must visit every playable node on the board exactly once in a single connected path, moving only in orthogonal steps.

Do I have to finish the path on a specific node?

Campaign, Daily and Weekly boards normally use a free end. Bonus Paths are the exception: they mark a specific goal node and limit how many times you can undo.

How many levels does the campaign have?

There are 200 campaign levels spread across four difficulties: Easy, Medium, Hard and Very Hard. A full level-select screen lets you filter by difficulty and by solved, unsolved or locked status.

Is the daily challenge the same for everyone?

Yes — each UTC day, all players get the same five levels. Your first completed run is ranked; later runs are practice and cannot overwrite that leaderboard attempt.

Can I challenge a friend?

Yes. Create a five-level friend challenge, finish your run, then share its code or cloud link. Your friend plays the identical boards and gets a split-by-split comparison.

Does One Stroke work on phones and tablets?

Yes. Just drag your finger from node to node to draw the path. A bottom tab bar and dedicated touch buttons for Undo, Reset, Hint and Next make one-handed play comfortable on any screen.
