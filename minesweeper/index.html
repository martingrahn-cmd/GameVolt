How to Play Minesweeper

Each cell is either safe or hides a mine. Reveal a safe cell and you'll see a number from 1 to 8 telling you exactly how many mines surround that cell. Use those numbers to deduce where the mines are. Flag suspected mines, reveal everything else, and the board is won.

  • Left-click (or tap) to reveal a cell.
  • Right-click (desktop) or long-press (mobile) to flag a mine.
  • Double-click a numbered cell to "chord" — auto-reveal its neighbours when the right number of flags is placed.
  • The first click is always safe and opens a cascade.

Minesweeper FAQ

Is this Minesweeper really free?

Yes. 100% free, no ads, no signup needed. Sign in only if you want cloud-synced trophies and a place on the global leaderboard.

What's the difference between no-guess mode and classic mode?

In classic mode, mines are placed randomly (with a safe zone around your first click). That sometimes creates "50/50" endgame positions where you must guess. No-guess mode runs a solver during board generation to guarantee a logical, guess-free solution — every board is winnable by pure deduction.

How does chording work?

Once a numbered cell is revealed, you can double-click it to "chord" — if the number of flagged neighbours matches the cell's number, all other neighbours are revealed instantly. This is the fastest way to clear large open areas. On mobile, tap a revealed number to chord.

Is the daily challenge the same for everyone?

Yes — every player worldwide gets the same daily board, generated from a date-seeded random number generator. Compare your times on the daily leaderboard.

Does it work on phones and tablets?

Yes. Tap to reveal, long-press to flag, or use the floating flag toggle for one-tap flagging. The board scales to your screen size, even on Hard.

What's the world record for Minesweeper Hard?

The current human world record for Hard (Expert) is just under 30 seconds. Beating 5 minutes unlocks the gold "Hard & Fast" trophy — a good benchmark for serious players.
