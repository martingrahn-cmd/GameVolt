Every variant comes with global leaderboards, shared statistics with win streaks, and 31 trophies to unlock across the whole collection. No download, no signup — pick a card and start playing.

Klondike Solitaire

Klondike is the classic solitaire everyone knows from their first computer. Draw cards from the stock, build the tableau in alternating colors, and move all 52 cards onto four foundation piles from Ace to King. Easy to learn, endlessly replayable — the most popular card game in the world.

FreeCell

FreeCell deals every card face-up, so the whole puzzle is visible from the first move. Four free cells give you temporary storage — use them wisely and nearly every deal is winnable. This is the thinker's solitaire, rewarding planning over luck.

Spider Solitaire

Spider challenges you to build descending sequences from King to Ace across ten tableau columns. Choose one, two or four suits to scale the difficulty from relaxing to genuinely brutal — winning a four-suit Spider game is one of the collection's gold-trophy feats.

Pyramid Solitaire

Pyramid turns solitaire into a matching puzzle: remove pairs of cards that add up to 13 until the pyramid-shaped layout is cleared. Quick arithmetic and a little foresight go a long way in this satisfying, fast-playing variant.

TriPeaks

TriPeaks is the arcade side of solitaire. Clear three peaks by picking cards one rank higher or lower than the current waste card, and chain moves together — long streaks earn big bonus points and their own trophies. Perfect for short, snappy sessions.

Golf Solitaire

Golf uses the same higher-or-lower rule as TriPeaks on a simple seven-column course. Clear the tableau in as few moves as possible — lower is better, just like real golf. The easiest variant to learn, with surprising depth for score chasers.

Which Solitaire Variant Should You Play?

  • New to solitaire? Start with Klondike or Golf — familiar rules and forgiving games.
  • Want pure strategy? FreeCell — full information, almost no luck.
  • Craving a challenge? Spider with two or four suits will test anyone.
  • Only have five minutes? TriPeaks or Pyramid are built for quick rounds.

Solitaire FAQ

Are these solitaire games really free?

Yes. All six games — Klondike, FreeCell, Spider, Pyramid, TriPeaks and Golf — are 100% free to play in your browser with no download or signup. Sign in only if you want your trophies synced across devices.

What is the difference between Klondike, FreeCell and Spider?

Klondike is the classic: draw from a stock and build foundations Ace to King with alternating colors. FreeCell deals everything face-up and gives you four free cells, making it a game of pure planning. Spider uses two decks and asks you to build King-to-Ace runs in the same suit, with one, two or four suits setting the difficulty.

Which solitaire variant is the easiest? Which is the hardest?

Golf is the easiest to pick up — one simple higher-or-lower rule. Klondike, Pyramid and TriPeaks sit in the middle. FreeCell demands the most thought, and four-suit Spider is the hardest game in the collection.

Do the games work on phones and tablets?

Yes. Every variant supports touch — tap and drag cards just like with a mouse. The card layouts resize to fit your screen, so the collection plays well on phones, tablets and desktops.

Are there leaderboards and achievements?

Yes. Each variant has global leaderboards for score and time, and the whole collection shares one trophy set: 31 trophies across bronze, silver, gold and platinum tiers, from your first win to winning all six variants and building long win streaks.

Is every deal winnable?

Not always — that's part of classic solitaire. Nearly all FreeCell deals are solvable because every card is visible from the start. Klondike, Spider, Pyramid, TriPeaks and Golf deal randomly, so some games can't be won; if you get stuck, just deal a new game.
